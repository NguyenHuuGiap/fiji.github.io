The difference you observe is because when converting from 16 to 8 bits, the image gets rescaled to still represent the range of values of the original. This has no relation to inverting an image. It is not a bug.
 
One suggestion is that if you need the original values, do not convert to 8bits and measure from the original image.

Comment 5 G. Landini 2011-06-06 00:54:10 CDT 
To answer why the average value of an image and its inverted version are different, have a look a the histogram of the two images.

Taking this to the extreme, if your image is all black (0), its average is 0. If you invert it, (let's say now it is 65535, in 16 bits) the average value will be 65535.

You can't expect the inverted image to have the same value, unless there was some special case of symmetrical histogram centred on the greyscale space or something like that.

Of course the standard deviation will be the same, because of the nature of the statistic. (You know what the standard deviation means, and how it is computed, don't you?)
